--- layout: post title: Bilingual Programmers, why knowing multiple languages isn't as hard as you think. category: programming excerpt: Most programmers end up programming in a myriad of different languages during their career and here's why it's not as intimidating as you think it is. ---

Most progammers starting out are affraid of the idea of having to learn another language. Here the general reason why:

  1. They believe what they've learned in their other language won't apply to the next.
  2. They believe data types are a thing only native to their first language of choice.
  3. They are like me and want "focus on mastering at 1 language first"
  4. They've only just begun tooo liiiivvvveeeeeeee or start proramming as everyone else puts it.
  5. Due to technical difficulties (lack of coffee) I cannot think of another reason but there are plenty.

The thing is, most beginner programmers, like myself are still very intimidated by programming so our rational minds fail to help us take a breath and realize that the large majority of computer science fundamentals apply to ANY LANGUAGE
